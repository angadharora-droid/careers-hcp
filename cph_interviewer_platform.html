Scoring Framework

SectionWeightCompetenciesWhy it's weighted this way
Attitude60%Guest Orientation, Cultural Fit, Communication, Learning & TeamworkPer CHR brief: attitude & behaviour predict hospitality success more than technical knowledge, which can be trained.
Skills25%Practical assessment, Problem Solving, Grooming & Presence, PMS/ComputerDemonstrable ability the candidate should already have some of.
Knowledge15%Role Knowledge, Hospitality AwarenessLowest weight — most teachable during induction.
Every competency is scored by behavioural MCQ, never a bare number. The five levels map to Exceptional 100% · Strong 80% · Acceptable 60% · Below Expectations 40% · Not Suitable 20% of that competency's weight.

Panel Composition Rule

Non-manager roles (levels B, C): 2 independent panellists. Manager roles (levels A1, A3 — GM, Ops Manager, Admin Head, Executive Chef): 3-member committee. Each scores separately on their own device/sheet; scores are compared, not merged during the interview.
